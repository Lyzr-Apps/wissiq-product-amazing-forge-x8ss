1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

WissIQ Technologies Inc. is a B2B SaaS company providing AI-powered financial intelligence solutions to mid-market and enterprise clients. Founded in 2019, the company is headquartered in San Francisco, California, with a satellite office in Austin, Texas.

The company completed its Series B funding round in March 2023, raising $18M at a $72M pre-money valuation, led by Acme Ventures with participation from existing investors TechCapital Partners and Early Stage Fund I.

Current Capitalization:
- Series A: $6M (2021) — TechCapital Partners lead
- Series B: $18M (2023) — Acme Ventures lead
- Total Equity Raised: $24M
- Convertible Notes Outstanding: $0
- Term Loan: $1.2M (Silicon Valley Bank, 36-month term, 7.5% interest)

As of September 2024, the company employs 58 full-time employees across four departments:
- Engineering: 37 FTEs (63.8%)
- Sales & Marketing: 26 FTEs (includes 4 SDRs, 8 AEs, 6 CSMs, 8 marketing)
- Operations & G&A: 15 FTEs

2. BUSINESS MODEL & REVENUE STREAMS

WissIQ operates a multi-tiered SaaS subscription model with three pricing tiers:

a) Enterprise Tier ($6,500-$8,500/month per account)
   - Full platform access with custom integrations
   - Dedicated customer success manager
   - SLA guarantees (99.95% uptime)
   - Custom reporting and API access
   - Current accounts: 50 (as of Sep 2024)
   - Average contract value: $7,200/month
   - Average contract duration: 24 months

b) Pro Tier ($750-$1,200/month per account)
   - Core platform features
   - Standard integrations
   - Email support with 4-hour SLA
   - Current accounts: 201 (as of Sep 2024)
   - Average contract value: $820/month
   - Average contract duration: 12 months

c) Starter Tier ($100-$200/month per account)
   - Basic platform access
   - Self-service onboarding
   - Community support
   - Current accounts: 400 (as of Sep 2024)
   - Average contract value: $126/month
   - Average contract duration: Month-to-month

Additional Revenue Streams:
- Professional Services: Implementation, onboarding, and custom development
  - Average implementation fee: $12,000-$18,000 (Enterprise)
  - Average onboarding fee: $2,500-$5,000 (Pro)
  - Gross margin on PS: ~45%

- Licensing: On-premise deployment licenses and API premium access
  - Annual license fee: $15,000-$65,000
  - API premium access: $5,000-$8,000/month

- Other: Training workshops ($3,500/session), consulting ($250/hour), partner referral commissions

Revenue Recognition Policy:
- SaaS subscriptions: Recognized ratably over the contract period (ASC 606)
- Professional services: Recognized upon delivery/completion
- Licenses: Recognized at point of delivery for perpetual; ratably for term licenses
- Note: Q3 2024 has a revenue recognition concern — three large contracts totaling $320K were recognized in September despite Q4 service delivery schedules. This requires audit review for ASC 606 compliance.

7. RISK FACTORS

Financial Risks (as of Sep 2024):

a) Margin Compression Risk (HIGH)
   Gross margins have declined from 72.1% to 68.5% over 6 months. If the trend continues, profitability could be threatened. Root causes include rising COGS (API costs, infrastructure), engineering overtime, and slower revenue-per-employee growth.
   Mitigation: Infrastructure optimization initiative, API cost renegotiation, overtime reduction plan.

b) Customer Concentration Risk (MEDIUM)
   Top 5 enterprise clients represent 28% of total ARR. Loss of any single top-5 client would materially impact revenue.
   Mitigation: Diversification through Pro tier growth, multi-year contracts with renewal incentives.

c) Marketing Spend Efficiency Risk (HIGH)
   Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) has increased 34% YoY from $895 to $1,200, significantly above the industry benchmark of $950. Marketing spend has exceeded budget by 18.3% without proportional revenue growth.
   Mitigation: Marketing channel audit, agency contract review, shift to lower-CAC channels.

d) Revenue Recognition Risk (CRITICAL — Q3 2024)
   Three contracts totaling $320K were recognized in Q3 2024 (September) despite service delivery scheduled for Q4. This requires immediate audit review for ASC 606 compliance. If reversed, Q3 revenue would be overstated by approximately 5.9%.
   Mitigation: Immediate internal audit, engagement of external auditors for independent review.

e) Cash Flow Timing Risk (LOW-MEDIUM)
   DSO of 45 days exceeds industry benchmark of 38 days. While cash runway is adequate at 14 months, deteriorating collections could compress runway to under 12 months.
   Mitigation: Automated invoice reminders, early payment discounts, dedicated collections FTE.

f) Data Quality Risk (LOW)
   12 expense entries in September lack proper category classification. Date format inconsistencies (MM/DD/YYYY vs YYYY-MM-DD) affect 45 imported records. A duplicate payment of $12,500 to CloudHost Inc was detected in June.
   Mitigation: Data governance process improvement, AP reconciliation automation.

g) Interest Rate / Debt Risk (LOW)
   The SVB term loan is at a fixed 7.5% rate, eliminating variable rate exposure. Debt-to-equity ratio of 0.33 is conservative and well within covenants.
